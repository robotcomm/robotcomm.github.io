Frequently Asked Questions

Identity Finder helps to prevent data loss by finding and protecting sensitive information. When Identity Finder locates confidential data in a file, the program tools can be used to remove the data by securely deleting the whole file or redacting data from the file.

Data handling policies are in place to protect the interests of university employees, students, affiliates and research subjects. In addition, improper handling of restricted information and personally-identifiable information (PII) may place the university at risk of breaching regulatory requirements.

If data files and the restricted information contained within are legitimately required for an approved purpose, contact Information Technology Services (216-368-HELP) to discuss secure storage and encryption options.

What is Identity Finder? ^
Identity Finder works like an anti-virus product. Client software installs locally, inspects storage media for data matching certain pre-defined criteria, and logs the activity with a management console operated by the Information Security Office. When pattern matches are found (e.g., potential social security numbers, credit card numbers) Identity Finder presents several options to remove the data:

  • Delete and overwrite the file (Shred)
  • Redact information from the file (Scrub)
  • Prepare the file for move to a secure location (Quarantine)
  • Safeguard the file using proprietary encryption (Secure)
In the case of a 'false positive' match users may also instruct Identity Finder to whitelist a file or location (Ignore).

Where can I find a copy of Case Western Reserve University’s SSN Policy? ^
Please visit www.case.edu/its/policy/ssn-use-policy.html to review Case Western Reserve's social security number policy.

What types of data are considered Restricted information? ^
  • legal investigations conducted by the university
  • internal audit and compliance data (integrity)
  • tenure committee working data (pre-selection)
  • electronic personal health information (ePHI)
  • social security numbers when associated with a person's name
  • birth date when associated with a person's name
  • credit card transaction data
  • Case Network ID in combination with associated passwords (note that the Case Network ID by itself is Public information)
  • intellectual property, trade secrets, technical data supporting technology transfer agreements (before public disclosure decisions have been made)
  • proprietary information entrusted to the University by agreements with third parties
  • detailed information pertaining to university incidents which must not be disclosed until approved by Marketing & Communications‌‌
  • any information designated in writing by the VP of Information Technology/CIO

Does Identity Finder work with Mac? ^
Yes. Identity Finder works with Mac OS X versions 10.4 and higher on Intel-based hardware systems.

How much configuration must I do after installation of Identity Finder? ^
None. The ITS staff at Case has already created default settings for finding restricted data on your machine.

What do I do with the results after the scan is complete? ^
Users are expected to protect all SSN data revealed by scan results as required by Case Western Reserve policy.
Review each individual result and make an appropriate handling decision. The Secure, Quarantine and Recycle options should only be used when specifically directed to do so by Information Security or its designated proxy.

What is a 'false positive' match? ^
Data incorrectly identified as PII or Restricted Information (e.g., Identity Finder may find a series of random, meaningless numbers in a file that appear in the same format as a credit card or social security number) is known as a ”false positive”.

This data does not need to be removed. Use the 'Ignore' action to to allow Identify Finder to ignore false positives or alerts generated by the user's own PII.

What is 'Quarantine'? ^
Quarantine moves the file containing PII to a designated folder to be acted upon later. When a location containing PII has been identified but the entire file is absolutely necessary, the Quarantine feature can be used to relocate that data to a separate folder. The 'Quarantine' option should only be used when specifically directed to do so by Information Security or its designated proxy.

If I shred a file that I later need, is there any way to get it back? ^
No. Once you shred it, it is overwritten. You should look closely at data you shred. If you use Carbonite to back up your files, you may be able to recover shredded files, depending on the backup cycle.

What if my work depends on handling and storing files that contain Restricted data? ^
Case Western Reserve SSN Policy allows retention of certain sensitive information in limited cases when the data collected supports fundamental university functions (e.g., limited research applications, financial aid processing, etc.).

These processes may be subject to data governance oversight. Any individual who maintains Restricted data under these circumstances is responsible for ensuring that effective security measures are employed.

Workstation(s) must be configured to meet minimum security standards stated in both Tier I Controls and Tier III Controls. Information Technology Services provides tools to handle Restricted data consistently and appropriately.

What data are reported to the Management Console? ^
Each client reports the computer name, the time scans were run, and aggregate results about how much sensitive data was found, shredded, scrubbed, or encrypted. No sensitive data are sent to the server.

Can the Information Security Office view the data found by the Identity Finder scan? ^
No. While the existence and nature of the data is reported to the management console, the data itself and the individual to whom it belongs are not revealed.

Is Identity Finder available for students or for home use? ^
Students will be permitted one download of Identity Finder Home Edition. Faculty and staff who wish to purchase the software for personal use can obtain it through the Case Software Center for $10.00.

How do I find all the computers that are registered to me? ^
The Case Network Registration Lookup tool permits you to see what computers have been registered to your Case ID.
