iTunes 12 for Mac: Default iTunes U settings

You can choose default settings that apply to any course without its own settings.

Menu Settings

Check for new materials

Choose how often iTunes checks for new episodes of a course. If you choose Manually, you can check for new episodes by clicking Refresh at the bottom of your iTunes directory.

Materials to Keep

Choose how many episodes of a course you want to keep. To delete episodes manually, choose All Episodes. To keep each episode until you watch it and then automatically delete it, choose All Unplayed Episodes. To automatically delete all older episodes, choose Most Recent Episode or Last 2-10 Episodes.

Automatically Download

Choose whether to have iTunes automatically download all available episodes or only the most recent episode of a course. If you don’t want any materials to download automatically, choose Off.

iTunes 12 for Mac: Download educational media from iTunes U

iTunes U is a part of the iTunes Store that features free lectures, language lessons, audiobooks, and more, which you can download and enjoy on your iPod, iPhone, iPad, or computer. iTunes U is available to everyone, and offers more than 100,000 educational audio and video files from top universities, museums, public media organizations, and other cultural institutions from around the world.

If you have a free iCloud account, your iTunes U courses, notes, and current play position sync to the iTunes U app on your iOS devices.

Download courses and lessons from iTunes U

  1. Click the iTunes U button.
  2. Click iTunes Store.
  3. When you find a course or lesson that interests you, do one of the following:
    • Subscribe to a course: Click Subscribe Free. iTunes downloads the most recent lesson.
    • Download a lesson: Click the Free button next to the lesson.

NOTE: Some iTunes U courses might use materials that need to be purchased, such as books and videos.

Update or unsubscribe from an iTunes U course

  1. Click the iTunes U button.

  2. Do one of the following:

    • Update your course materials: Select a course and click the Refresh button next to the course title. All currently available lessons from the courses you subscribe to are displayed.
    • Unsubscribe from a course: Control-click a course and choose Unsubscribe Collection.

Choose your default iTunes U settings

  1. Click the iTunes U button.
  2. Click the Settings button, then click Defaults.

  3. Choose the settings you want:

    • Check for new materials: Choose how often to check for new lessons
    • Materials to Keep: Choose a maximum number of lessons to keep
    • Automatically Download: Choose whether you want new lessons to be downloaded automatically
  4. Click OK.

Choose settings for individual iTunes U courses

Each course you subscribe to can have its own settings.

  1. Click the iTunes U button.

  2. Select a course, click the Settings button and choose the settings you want:

    • Keep: Choose a maximum number of lessons to keep
    • Download: Choose which lessons to download
    • Sort Order: Choose order in which lessons are sorted
    • Play: Choose order in which lessons are played
  3. Click Done.

If you don’t choose settings for a course, your default settings will apply.

FAQ

See the frequently asked questions below to learn more about the iTunes U app and iTunes U catalog.

About the iTunes U app and the iTunes U Catalog

Where can I find the iTunes U app?

You can download the iTunes U app for free from the App Store on your iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch.

Is the iTunes U app available in every country with an App Store?

Yes, the iTunes U app and iTunes U content is available in all App Store countries.

What are the requirements to use the iTunes U app?

  • You can use the iTunes U app on iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch with iOS 5 and iTunes 10.5.2 or later.
  • You must have an iTunes Store account to download content from the iTunes U catalog.

How do I access the iTunes U catalog?

Use the iTunes U app to access the iTunes U catalog. Tap the iTunes U icon and your bookshelf appears. In the upper corner, tap the Catalog button and the bookshelf flips around to reveal the iTunes U catalog. From the catalog, browse through more than 800,000 free courses, lectures, videos, films, and other resources.

Managing your iTunes U content

Are courses and collections from the iTunes U catalog downloaded to my iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch?

Yes. When you tap the Download or Subscribe buttons, the iTunes U app will download that item to your bookshelf.

Can I download from the iTunes U catalog using my computer?

Yes, you can browse and download from the iTunes U catalog on your computer, but some content may only be available using the iTunes U app.

Can I back up the iTunes U content I download?

Yes, all content you’ve downloaded from the iTunes U catalog will be automatically added to your iTunes library the next time you sync your device with your computer.

Can I gift content from the iTunes U catalog?

No, you cannot gift content from the iTunes U catalog.

Is there a limit to the number of courses or collections I can have?

The number of courses or collections you can have in the iTunes U app varies depending on the amount of space available on your device. The iTunes U app does not limit the number of courses or collections allowed on your bookshelf.

What if I can’t find my instructor’s course in the iTunes U catalog?

If you’re unable to find your instructor’s course in the iTunes U catalog, you will need to contact your instructor for the course enrollment URL.

iTunes U app features

Can the iTunes U app play audio or video included with courses or collections?

Yes.

Can I print or email my notes and PDFs from the iTunes U app?

Yes, you can print PDFs and notes you’ve taken.

Does the iTunes U app include a dictionary?

Yes, you can look up the definition of words from the iTunes U app using a built-in English or Japanese language dictionary.

Can I create Notes?

The iTunes U app includes a built-in Notes tab. Create Course Notes or view Book Notes, Bookmarks, and Highlights you’ve made in books you’ve read from the course using the Notes tab. To view your Book Notes, Bookmarks, and Highlights, tap the Notes button and then tap Book Notes.

Can I rearrange courses on my iTunes U bookshelf?

Yes, you can rearrange courses in your iTunes U bookshelf by touching and holding a course, then moving the course where you would like it. You can also sort courses in the iTunes U app by switching to List view and tapping either Bookshelf, Titles, Providers, or Categories buttons.

Can I read my courses while my device is in airplane mode?

Yes, you do not need an Internet connection to read iTunes U content you’ve already downloaded.

Do courses from the iTunes U catalog have any accessibility features?

Courses downloaded from the iTunes U catalog make use of the accessibility features built into iPad, iPhone, and iPod touch.

Access and manage courses online with iTunes U

Learn how to access iTunes U, a web-based tool for instructors who want to create courses and manage lessons and assignments.

Before you begin, review these requirements:

  • Valid Apple ID (Learn more about Apple ID)
  • Safari 6.1 or later on a Mac
  • Mozilla Firefox 12 or later on a Mac or PC
  • Google Chrome 28 or later on a Mac or PC

Follow these steps to access iTunes U:

  1. Using your web browser, go to the iTunes U portal.
  2. Sign in using your Apple ID and password. If you don’t have an Apple ID, you can create one by clicking “create one now” on the login page.
  3. After you sign in, you can start creating courses.

While creating, designing, and editing your course, you can use the Enroll Code, under the Settings button on the dashboard, to copy a course link into an email, send the email to your iPad, iPhone, or iPod touch, and preview and test your course from a student’s perspective before sharing the course with others. When you’re ready to share your course, use the Enroll Code to share a link to your course with students and other users.
