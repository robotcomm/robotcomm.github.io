Preparing Annual Disclosures for Certification

As part of the research community, you are required to submit an annual outside financial interest disclosure form. Your disclosures are reviewed for potential conflict of interest and certified by committee. You must also update your disclosures as they change during the year.


You will receive a notification from SPARTA COI by email when disclosure materials are ready for your review. Sign in to SPARTA COI using your CWRU network ID and password to complete the disloser portion of the annual conflict of interest certification process.

Follow the on-screen instructions to submit your materials to the Office of Research Administration for review. Additional next steps will be provided by email, if needed.

Before you begin, gather the following:

  • Any consulting agreements you have signed this year
  • Receipts from travel paid for by outside companies
  • Any stock option agreements
  • Your stock portfolio summary
  • Your current IRS 1040 and 1099 forms

NOTE: you need not complete the process in one sitting. Choose to save your progress and submit the form at a later time if needed. Contact the Office of Research Administration for additional guidance.

Managing Individual Disclosures

You must disclose any financial relationship or interest you hold with a company or external organization. Each organization in which you hold an outside financial interest must be disclosed indvidually. However, multiple occurrences of financial interest with a single organization may be reported using a single disclosure form (e.g., Board of Trsutees and financial benefit from Intellectual Property).

Whether a particular disclosure meets criteria for additional review (i.e., is ‘significant’) is determined systematically. Significant disclosures undergo review by the Office of Research Administration or Office of General Counsel. The Office of Research Administration will notify disclosers of any next needed by email.


Example: email response, Annual Disclosure form reviewed; no actionable conflict of interest


To:     
Subject:        COMMITTEE REVIEW COMPLETE: NO ISSUES FOUND
Link:       
Dear  :

Your  Outside Financial Interests Disclosure Form has been reviewed.

Although, you have reported an outside financial interest, it is not considered to be an actionable conflict of interest.  No further follow up is necessary at this time.

Please update your Disclosure Form within 30 days of discovering or acquiring a new outside financial interest, or within 30  days of elimination of a previously disclosed outside financial interest.

At the beginning of each year, you will be asked to complete the annual Disclosure Form again for the entire previous calendar year.

For more information, please reply to the Conflict of Interests Committee staff email caseoic@case.edu or you may contact the Coordinator, Yovonda Rease, by telephone at 216-368-5963.

Thank you for your cooperation!


You may also choose to indicate that you have no outside financial interests to dislcose.

Edit my certification

All new SPARTA COI users are required to document disclosures completed prior to the implementation of SPARTA COI in the new system. However, after disclosures are added to SPARTA COI, they will carry over year-to-year as needed.


  1. Sign in to SPARTA COI using your CWRU network ID and password
  2. Click Home
  3. Find the Inbox link at the upper-right of the screen
  4. Click the name of your annual disclosure form (as directed by email instruction)
  5. From the disclosure certification workspace, click Edit my Certification
    6. NOTE: If “My Current Actions” displays “Create My Certification”, then annual disclosure materials may not yet be prepared. Consult the Office of Research Administration before continuing.
  6. Review the Training and Education materials provided
  7. Select confirmation checkbox to indicate that you have read and understand Training and Education materials
  8. Review the Introduction and CWRU Policy on Conflict of Interests (link provided).
  9. Click Continue
  10. Review the types of interests that must be disclosed.
  11. If any interests must be disclosed for yourself or a family member, choose “Yes”. Otherwise, choose “No”

  12. Click Continue

Complete Annual Certification Without Disclosures

If you choose to complete your annual conflict of interest form (COI) without disclosing any outside financial interests,


  1. Provide any clarifying information or documents using the space provided.
  2. If none, leave blank.
  3. Click Continue
  4. Review “Assurance and Certification” statements
  5. Click confirmation checkbox to indicate agreement
  6. Click Finish to submit the form, or Back to make corrections
  7. You will receive a confirmation email after certification is complete, or requesting changes if needed

Add a New Disclosure

If you have new outside financial interest(s) to disclose (see Conflict of Interest Compliance website for definitions and examples) complete the following,


  1. Click + Add Disclosure
  2. Begin typing the name of organization with which you have a relationship
  3. Click the name of the organization when it appears to select it.
  4. If the name of organization does not appear, click Select to search using advanced options
  5. If name still does not appear, type it into the text box provided
  6. Click OK
  7. Choose the organization type from options provided
  8. Choose relationship type(s) from options provided
  9. Choose total amount of compensation provided to you for services from ranges provided (if none, choose “0”)
  10. Click Continue
  11. If organization does not exist in database, you will be asked to provide a one sentence description of the organization’s purpose (e.g., what it does)
  12. You will be asked to provide details about each relationship type you specified previously and whether the relationship is held by yourself, a family member or both. See, Relationship Questions in Detail
  13. Click Continue
  14. You will be asked to describe the business and research relationship(s) between yourself and selected organization. See, Business and Research Relationships in Detail
  15. Review Disclosure Summary
  16. Click Finish to submit disclosure for selected organization or Back to make changes
  17. Completed disclosure is now listed under section 1, Disclosures Under Review
  18. To add an additional, previously undisclosed outside interest, click + Add Disclosure
  19. You will receive a confirmation email after certification is complete, requesting changes if needed or outlining required next steps.
  20. Disclosers may also monitor the status of disclosure certification by signing in SPARTA COI and selecting “My Disclosures” from the “Shortcuts” menu at the left of the screen. Select your annual disclosure and review the “Summary” section.


Previously reviewed disclosures

All new SPARTA COI users are required to document disclosures completed prior to the implementation of SPARTA COI in the new system. However, after disclosures are added to SPARTA COI, they will carry over year-to-year as needed.

Disclosures submitted during prior certification cycles are listed by organization under section 2, Previously Reviewed Disclosures.


  1. Click Modify to change or confirm each previously reviewed disclosure
  2. If a previously disclosed outside interest no longer applies, click the ☒ control to the right of the entry to deactivate it

Editing Previously Reviewed Disclosures

If the nature of your relationship changes with an organization, you might need to go back and modify a prior disclosure of outside financial interest. All of your disclosures are displayed on the Disclosure Details form.

As soon as you modify a previously reviewed disclosure, it becomes part of the current certification process and is subject to review.

Removing Individual Disclosures

When you no longer have any payments or equity with an organization, you can deactivate the disclosure associated with it.

When you deactivate a disclosure, it is not immediately removed from the system. An ORA analyst must approve the deactivation before it is removed from your set of disclosures.

You may deactivate a disclosure at any time.


Edit draft disclosures

You might not have all the necessary information needed to complete a conflict of interest disclosure. Save your progress at any time and return later to complete it.

Until a certification is submitted for review, the information is editable.

  1. Sign in to SPARTA COI
  2. Click Home
  3. Find the Inbox tab at the upper-right of the screen
  4. In the Filter By selection menu, choose State
  5. Type “Draft” in the entry box next to the selection menu
  6. Click Go
  7. Editable drafts are shown in My Inbox
  8. Click the name of the draft you would like to edit
  9. Click Edit to add or modify details as needed. See, Edit my certification

If the information you want to update is part of a specific disclosure, select Disclosure Details from the “Jump To” list at the top the of page.


Edit disclosures under review

If a disclosure is under review and the review is in a state where you can modify it, the disclosure appears with an Edit button next to it. If a disclosure is under review, but the review is in a state where someone else is working on it, the disclosure appears with a View button next to it and you cannot make changes.


Complete requested changes

You will receive a notification by email if reviewers request changes to your disclosure before completing certification.

  1. Sign in to SPARTA COI
  2. Click Home
  3. Find the Inbox tab at the upper-right of the screen
  4. Find the name of your disclosure certification project and click to open
  5. From the disclosure certification workspace, click Edit Disclosures
  6. Make requested changes to disclosures and certification forms.
  7. When done, save and exit
  8. In the disclosure certification workspace, click Submit Changes

Mid-year Updates to Previously Reviewed Disclosures

If your interests change during the year, you must update your disclosures to reflect those changes. Depending on a number of factors, those changes may or may not need to be reviewed and certified by the Office of Research Administration.

  1. Sign in to SPARTA COI
  2. Click COI
  3. Click Create My Certification
  4. Briefly describe the reason you are updating your disclosures
  5. Click OK to continue or Cancel to abandon
  6. Click Continue to move through disclosure review forms.
  7. When done, save and exit

For more information on completing disclosure certification forms, see Edit my certification.

Business and Research Relationships in Detail

Research Relationships

  1. Describe specific research relationships between selected organization, yourself and the university

  2. Choose from the following:

    • This organization sponsors my research
    • This organization supports my research (e.g., provides biologics, devices, drugs, equipment, technical expertise or other services)
    • This organization manufactures products used in my research
    • This organization has intellectual property rights or holds a license that will be utilized, developed, or studied in my research
    • This organization is a Research Collaborator (e.g., sub-recipient, Small Business Innovation Research [SBIR], Small Business Technology Research [STTR])
    • I have no research relationship with this organization

Business Relationships

  1. Describe specific business relationships between selected organization, yourself and the university

  2. Choose from the following:

    • I make purchasing decisions with this organization for the university
    • I make purchasing decisions with a competitor of this organization involved in my research
    • I have a business relationship with this organization
    • I have a business relationship with a competitor of this organization involved in my research
    • I have no business, purchasing and/or vendor relationship with this organization

Definitions

All CWRU COI Policies and Definitions
Clinical affiliates
  • University Hospitals Case Medical Center (UHCMC),
  • Cleveland Clinic,
  • MetroHealth Medical Center (MHMC), and
  • Louis Stokes Cleveland Veterans Affairs Medical Center (LSCVAMC)
Equity
Any stock, stock option or other ownership interest in an outside entity
Exclusions
activities in which individuals may engage without disclosure additional information
Family Member
defined as yourself, your spouse/domestic partner, dependent children, and any other dependent person living in the same household.
Outside Financial Interest
anything of monetary value, whether or not the value is readily ascertainable
Institutional / University Responsibilities
Professional responsibilities conducted on behalf of the University (e.g., research, research consultation, teaching, professional practice, institutional committee memberships, and service on panels such as Institutional Review Boards or Data and Safety Monitoring Boards)
Intellectual Property Rights and Interests
intellectual property rights and interests (e.g., patents, copyrights) except intellectual property rights assigned to the institution and agreements to share in royalties related to such rights.
Investigator
An investigator for purposes of financial interest disclosure is any personnel listed on any sponsored project through CWRU (not only research, but also including administrative, training, fellowship or assessment projects), and/or an investigator conducting research at CWRU or one of its clinical affiliates.
No Actionable Conflict of Interests
Individuals who have an outside interest that is not significant, or is found to be unrelated to University research or responsibilities, will not require further action regarding their outside interest. Such individuals’ annual outside interest review will be marked as "no actionable conflict of interest."
Officer or Director
For purposes of outside financial interest disclosure, the "officer or director" holds a board membership or other officer position involving advisory or fiduciary duties with any outside entity whether uncompensated or not.
Outside Entity
For purposes of financial interest disclosure, an "outside entity" is any corporation, partnership, proprietorship, firm, association, organization, or other entity outside the University and its clinical affiliates (but excluding federal, state or local government agencies). If the disclosing individual has received disclosable remuneration or other financial interest from a person, then the person is also an "entity" by this definition.
Population Required To Disclose
Any individual pertaining to one or more of the following populations, Faculty, Investigators and Senior Staff, is required to disclose per CWRU COI policy.
Faculty
denotes Board of Trustees-appointed full, associate, or assistant professor; senior instructor or instructor
Senior staff
university officer, senior official or staff member. "Senior officials" required to complete the form comprise the Provost, Senior Vice President for Administration, Chief Financial Officer, Vice President for Medical Affairs, Chief of Staff, vice presidents for Development, University Relations, and Diversity, Inclusion and Equal Opportunity, and any other person that the President designates. Staff with purchasing / vendor responsibilities are required to complete the form (as designated by salary grade and purchasing limit), and any other employees at the request of their supervisors.
Travel
Investigators must disclose the occurrence of any reimbursed or sponsored travel related to an individual’s institutional responsibilities
Routine Follow-up
Routine follow-up is review that is intended to assist the investigator and the University in fulfilling the CWRU policy as well as NIH regulations–to identify and manage any financial conflicts of interest with research. The Conflict of Interests Committee staff will work with the investigator to summarize their financial interests and related University responsibilities for the COIC. Where the COIC determines there could be the appearance of a conflict, the Committee works with the investigator to manage the conflict.
Salary
Disclosable salary and remuneration is salary and any payment for services not otherwise identified as salary (e.g., consulting fees, honoraria, paid authorship).
Significant Financial Interest ("SFI")
In a research context, Significant Financial Interest ("SFI") means a financial interest of the Investigator (and those of the Investigator’s spouse and dependent children, domestic partner or anyone else living in the same household), that reasonably appears to be related to the Investigator’s institutional responsibilities. When outside multiple financial interests for the same outside entity or source are disclosed, the information will be reviewed in aggregate for significance.
