Instructions

Carefully read the Case Description (labeled "UCx") and "Basic Flow" instructions. Access the EvaluationKit test environment and follow the steps provided. After you have completed the task, rate your experience by responding to the three (3) questions that follow the step-by-step instructions.

Each of the eleven (11) use cases represents a single, common task. You may submit your responses only after rating the instructions for each task. Please note that your responses are NOT anonymous. However, your frank advice is appreciated. All comments will be shared with the project team and considered carefully.

UC1 - Student reviews response data for any section

NOTE: Students may access data associated with any section(s) that has reported results.

Basic Flow

  1. Sign in to case.evaluationkit.com with CWRU network ID and password.
  2. Click "Student Reporting", shown at top of screen.
  3. Select at least one "Area".
  4. Click Search to show available results in "Reports" grid.
  5. Choose a report from results and click its name to produce a PDF copy of the report.
  6. The system will prompt the user to open the file, or save it locally.

⚠ For the final step, please note that behavior may vary based on your browser settings.




  • For project team use only
    Related Requirements:
    Primary Actor(s): Student
    Preconditions: survey closed prior to report access, data available in system

UC4 - Instructor reviews results for a section after course evaluation survey has closed

NOTE: Instructors may only access data associated with a section(s) he or she teaches.

Basic Flow

  1. Click link to survey results included in notification email.
  2. Sign in to case.evaluationkit.com with CWRU network ID and password.
  3. Choose Results (in the upper-right corner) and then Instructor Results from dropdown menu.
  4. The "Instructor Results" page displays.
  5. Locate section by title in the "Results" grid.
  6. Click the download icon in the "View" column.
  7. Choose a report format. Use either "PDF" or "Raw Data" (excel - .xslx) to review results WITHOUT comments.
  8. The system will generate requested report and prompt the user to open the file, or save it locally.

⚠ For the final step, please note that behavior may vary based on your browser settings. Also, in certain cases, the system may email you a link to the report after processing is completed, rather than downloading it immediately.




  • For project team use only
    Related Requirements: 2
    Primary Actor(s): Instructor
    Preconditions: survey closed prior to report access, data available in system, results notification received by instructor
    Trigger: Instructor is notified by email that survey results are available.

UC9 - Administrator or designee (e.g., assistant) collects all results for a cross-listed course

NOTE: Administrators and their designees (e.g., assistant) may only access data associated with academic organization(s) he or she administers. Organizational relationships reflect SIS.

Basic Flow

EK does not automatically combine results of cross-listed courses. Instead, they are treated as separate entities. Results from cross-listed courses that fall within the same project (e.g., use the same questions) may be combined using batch reporting, provided that the individual producing the report has access to all areas within the project hierarchy where data for cross-listings are stored.

  1. Sign in to case.evaluationkit.com with CWRU network ID and password.
  2. Choose Results (in the upper-right corner) and then Project Results from dropdown menu.
  3. The "Project Results" page displays.
  4. Scroll through records or search for project by name to find desired project.
  5. Click magnifying glass in the "View" column to open detail page.
  6. Click the tab titled "By Course Section".
  7. The "Project Course Section Results" page opens.
  8. Limit results by searching sections, if needed.
  9. Click the checkbox next to course code for each section to include in report.
  10. Click the "Batch Report" link.
  11. Choose report format, "Aggregate Data for Selected Items into One Report" and click GO. A report is generated and sent to the user.

Example:

Students may enroll in a fictional course entitled "Technical Communication for Software Engineers". One section is available for Spring semester, 2015. The course is listed as both ENGL 1201 [title] and COMM 1201 [title].

To produce a report that includes survey results from students enrolled under both designations, an individual will need to have access to both the CAS > English and CAS > Communication Science sections of the survey project.




  • For project team use only
    Related Requirements: 2
    Primary Actor(s): Instructor
    Preconditions: survey responses completed, survey closed, data available in system
    Triggers: instructor receives email with link to survey results

UC10 - Administrator or designee (e.g., assistant) reports results of a project for review

NOTE: Administrators and their designees (e.g., assistant) may only access data associated with academic organization(s) he or she administers. Organizational relationships reflect SIS.

Basic Flow

  1. Sign in to case.evaluationkit.com with CWRU network ID and password.
  2. Choose Results (in the upper-right corner) and then Project Results from dropdown menu.
  3. The "Project Results" page displays.
  4. Scroll through records or search for project by name to find desired project.
  5. Click magnifying glass in the "View" column to open detail page.
  6. The "Project Hierarchy Level Results" page opens.
  7. Click the label named Select Level in the "Hierarchy Level" box and choose an academic organization from the list (e.g., Mathematics).
  8. Click the "Batch Report" link.
  9. Choose report format according to current needs.
    • PDF Report - Results for all reporting sections in an academic organization are combined in one PDF. Comments are excluded.
    • PDF Report + Comments - Results for all reporting sections in an academic organization are combined in one PDF. Comments are included.
    • Raw Data - Data for each response is exported in Microsoft Excel (.xlsx) format.
  10. Click GO. A report including comments is generated.
Raw Data Example:
Path,CourseCode,CourseTitle,UniqueID,InstructorName,Enrollments,Question 1
