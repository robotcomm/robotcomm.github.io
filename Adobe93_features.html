Adobe Connect 9.3, Features Summary

Feature changes for Adobe Connect, version 9.3 are summarized below. For additional details and technical specifications please refer to vendor-provided release notes.

Screen Sharing Changes

Control Panel
Redesigned screen sharing Control Panel provides presenter with tools to control the meeting without having to end screen sharing or go back to the meeting room.
  • To move the panel, click and drag the top bar
  • The option to revert to full meeting room is located at the right corner of the panel
  • Minimize the panel by clicking the icon of the current open view or double-clicking the top bar of the panel
Previewing
Screen sharing option menu is redesigned to provide additional visual feedback:
  • Blocked and blacklisted applications are not shown
  • Selected applications and windows are highlighted
Attendee controls
Granular attendee management provides host with access to change attendee roles, grant rights and more.
Input / Output
  • Fully functional web cam pod - start, pause and stop web cam feeds
  • Speaker and Microphone controls let you change the volume, mute and unmute your audio input/output (I/O) devices
Stepped zoom
Users will now have more zoom level options available to them than the two levels available currently.
  • Number of steps available varies by screen size.
  • Stepped zoom is not available to a presenter who is screen sharing.
Notifications
  • Allows delegation of notification management
  • Shows all pending notifications

White Board Changes

Changes to whiteboard capabilities fall broadly into three categories - granting drawing rights to participants, improved fidelity and exporting drawings.

Drawing tools
  • Palette is vertically oriented and larger
  • Menus open horizontally
Pens
  • New look for marker, highlighter, pen, and pencil
  • New pens exhibit more lifelike behavior
Color picker
  • Tool interface resembles other Adobe drawing tools
  • Pick colors by selecting hue, saturation, and brightness values with vertical sliders
Arrange menu
  • Select objects and move them in front of or behind other objects
  • Icons represent Move to Front, Move Forward, Move Backward, and Move to Back
Default settings
Adobe Connect 9.3 will remember your settings for whiteboards and documents within your meeting room.
New Permissions
Hosts can grant drawing rights to participants in one of three ways:
  1. Allow individual users to draw - select name(s) from the attendee list. Hover over participant name reveal privilege options
  2. Allow everyone to draw on a SELECTED share pod or whiteboard - select Allow Participants to draw from the context menu of that pod
  3. Allow everyone to draw on ANY share pod or whiteboard - set preference for the meeting room
Exporting drawings
Select Export Snapshot from the context menu in the pod to save your drawing locally or send it to others
Migration
Whiteboard objects from previous versions of Adobe Connect will be migrated to Connect version 9.3
Deprecated
  • Drop shadows
  • ‘3D look’ (skeuomorph) stamps

Miscellaneous Changes

Chat sounds
Any user may enable audible notifications for new messages received in a selected chat pod.
Attendee status
Hosts in the main meeting room will be able to see the status of all users in break out rooms. Notifications sent by participants using the “Send message to host” option persist until the host chooses to respond or dismiss it.
Save recordings
When a user tries to delete a meeting room, he or she will be prompted to move all recordings to the My Content section. This option is selected by default but is not mandatory.
Allow microphone
There are two locations from which all participants may be given Microphone rights at once:
  1. Any time from Preference > Audio Conference
  2. From the audio menu, once the conference is started
Settings selected using Preference > Audio Conference are now remembered across user sessions.
Webcam rights
Participants may only start / stop his or her own webcam feed.
Bandwidth control
This release provides greater control for Administrators to decide the optimum usage of bandwidth in the meeting rooms of their account.
  • Admins may set the default values for Screen Share and Video quality to be applicable for all meeting rooms in the account
  • Default values may be changed by meeting hosts
  • Admins may choose to lock bandwidth limits
This option is available under Administration > Compliance and Control > Share Settings.
