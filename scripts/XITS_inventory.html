Service Description

The ITS Inventory process (Inventory) is the process by which ITS describes and catalogs its personal computing hardware resources.

The Inventory includes currently assigned hardware that within its useful lifecycle, newly acquired hardware, peripherals (e.g., secondary monitors, external drives, etc.), accessories (e.g., cabling, stands, wireless input devices, etc.), network appliances and mobile devices.

Personally owned equipment of any kind is not included in the Inventory. Software (regardless of license) is not included in the Inventory.

Requests to assign spares from the Inventory, enter new equipment into the Inventory or complete hardware refreshes are fulfilled by DSS during general business hours only.

Requests to retrieve employee equipment due to termination or resignation are fulfilled by DSS during general business hours only, unless a manager, leadership or HR determines that the matter is critical / urgent.

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

Escalation is not available for this service.

Escalation procedures

ITS Inventory Process is not considered a critical service. Escalation is not available for this service.

Help locating serial numbers / service tags

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Refer to directions that apply to user equipment.

  3. Dell laptop

    1. Close the screen of your Dell Latitude laptop.
    2. Turn the laptop over.
    3. Look for a five-to seven-character code that includes numbers and letters. The code is located near the middle of the back of the computer, on a thin white strip. This is the serial number, also referred to as the service tag.

  4. Chromebook

    1. Close the screen of your Chromebook.
    2. Turn the Chromebook over.
    3. You can find the serial number on a sticker at the bottom of your Chromebook, underneath the bar code. The serial number can be up to 22 characters, depending on your manufacturer.

  5. Macintosh notebook

    1. Refer to Apple Support article #ht1559.
    2. This covers MacBook Pro, but also applies generally to most Apple laptops.

  6. iPhone, iPad, iPod Touch

    1. Refer to Apple Support article #ht4061
    2. This article also provides detailed instructions for locating other device-specific information: IMEI/MEID, ICCID, Model #, OS build, memory capacity, et al.

  7. Thunderbolt Display, Apple Cinema Display

    1. Refer to Apple Support article #HT3249
    2. Commonly, serial numbers for Apple Displays are located under the base or stand of the display.
    3. To view the tag, the user will need to lay the display on its face (place on a clean, soft towel or cloth on the surface to protect the screen).
    4. If the user has the original packaging, the serial number will be located on the exterior of the box.

  8. Apple magic mouse, keyboard

    • Open the battery hatch door on the bottom of the mouse, remove the batteries. The serial number will be located in the middle.
    • The serial number of the Apple Wireless Keyboard is located on the bottom of the keyboard. The exact location will vary depending on the style of keyboard. For aluminum keyboards, the serial number is located near the power button or centered between the power button and battery access cover.

  9. Android Phone, IMEI (15 characters)

    • By touching All Apps in the Favorites tray on any Home screen > Settings > About phone/About tablet > Status
    • By dialing *#06#
    • On the SIM card tray
    • On the device box
    • On the back of your device

  10. Android tablet, CSSN/serial number

    • By touching All Apps in the Favorites tray on any Home screen > Settings > About phone/About tablet > Status > Serial number
    • The serial number is on the back of your device
    • The CSSN number is on the device box

  11. Enter SN information into ticket, complete required information and resolve or forward to DSS resolver group as directed.

What items are inventoried

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.

  2. Any of the following hardware items or classes should be inventoried (if not specified, consult with Paul Kalanish).

    • Currently assigned hardware within its useful lifecycle,
    • Any newly acquired hardware,
    • Computer peripherals (e.g., secondary monitors, external drives, etc.),
    • Accessories (e.g., cabling, stands, wireless input devices, etc.),
    • Network appliances,
    • Mobile devices.
  3. Resolve the ticket or forward to DSS resolver group.


NOTE: Personally owned equipment of any kind is not included in the Inventory. Software titles (regardless of license) are also not included.

Move + Change

Submit a hardware refresh request

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Complete request with details provided by the requester.
  3. Note approver name (if not requester) and requester CWRU ID in ticket.
  4. Note description of equipment to be replaced, and whether or not old system has been entered into Inventory.
  5. Provide ticket number to requester.
  6. Forward to DSS resolver group.

NOTE: If needed, review the entire High Level process, start-to-finish.

Submit a hardware release / pickup request

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Complete request with details provided by the requester.
  3. Note in ticket whether request is described as urgent or critical.
  4. If described as urgent or critical, note under whose authority.
  5. Provide ticket number to requester.
  6. Forward to DSS resolver group.

NOTE: User equipment with access to Internal Use Only data or Restricted data, and the Box Sync client may require special handling. Please note these cases in the ticket so that DSS may process appropriately.
