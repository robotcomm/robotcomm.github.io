Service Description

ITS operates a high performance computing cluster to support computationally intense research and learning initiatives at the university. Cluster nodes run Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL).

Researchers are invited to participate in the growth of central high performance computing facilities by providing hardware, or by sharing software and operational costs. Operation of the facility is guided by usage policies approved by the Advisory Committee on Research Computing.

Use this article when:

  1. Any user has a general question about, wishes to contribute to, or requires consultation regarding High Performance Computing at CWRU,
  2. An account user has specific questions about his or her new/ongoing research and applications of the High Performance Computing Cluster, modifications to a group account, or billing,
  3. A non-account user has a question about securing High Performance Computing resources, establishing an account, or requests consultation regarding computationally intense new or ongoing research at CWRU.

NOTE: ALWAYS provide the user the followup email address “its-cluster-admin@case.edu” before resolving any ticket.

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Detailed Outage Procedures

return to top | index | outage questions

I suspect that the HPCC is down.

return to outage questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Check the service status in Solarwinds.
  3. Open the HPCC status webpage (see chart below).
    • Red Hat Enterprise Linux 5 (RHEL5)
    • Red Hat Enterprise Linux 6 (RHEL6)
  4. Type in your CWRU Network ID and password
  5. Check the date and time of the status to verify it is current.
  6. Basic information on how many nodes are available and running, how many nodes are down (intentionally or not) is summarized at top of page.
  7. If more than 50% of nodes are available, middle portion of page reads “HPCC status is OK”,
  8. If more than 50% of nodes are unavailable, middle portion of page reads “Possible Power Outage at HPCC”.
  9. Advise user of possible power outage and details(i.e. xx number of nodes are available…, etc.), provide followup email address “its-cluster-admin@case.edu”, and resolve ticket.
  10. The bottom section of the page specifies whether the head nodes (login and master) are accessible, the number of jobs running on the cluster, and current cluster use information.
  11. If the bottom section of the page reads “Moab is down, HPC status in question” advise user that HPCC is experiencing performance issues and immediately forward the ticket to the Research Computing resolver group.
