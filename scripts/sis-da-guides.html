Service Description

Student Information System (SIS) is an administrative database. SIS is the database of record for student information and the university course catalog.

SIS is available to all enrolled students and all faculty. Staff who support students, faculty, or academic administration should complete a security form to request access.

NOTE: A security form is required for ANY user who needs access to student data. There are three main access points for most campus users:

  1. Schedule of Classes - for departmental users who maintain each term’s course offerings
  2. Department Admin Center - academic, financial or basic biographical/demographic data (per student basis)
  3. Data Warehouse - preformatted reports using student data (NOTE: this system resides outside of SIS)

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within second-level support.

Who to contact for escalation

Stacy Mitchell, Email: sam57@case.edu, P: (216) 368-0094

Escalation procedures

SIS is a critical service. Escalations are available whenever requested, including during planned university closures and recognized university holidays.

During unplanned university closures escalations may NOT be available.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

back to top

  3. Displays classes currently taught by an instructor.
  4. Current semester classes are displayed when the Faculty Center is accessed.

Help with Grades and Grading Proxies

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Verify that the user is calling in his or her capacity as an administrator.
  3. Midterm and Final Grades, Grading Proxies
  4. Grading proxies are designated personnel authorized to enter midterm and final grades in the “Grade Roster screen”.
  5. To submit final grades, an instructor must access the grade roster and perform an approval process.
  6. Instructors and proxies may also change an incomplete grade.
  7. To become a grading proxy, an “Add/Update/Delete Instructor” form should be submitted to the University Registrar’s office.
  8. Provide end user with “Entering Final Grades”, “Entering Midterm Grades” and “Changing an Incomplete Grade” documents linked in table above if additional detail is requested. See, "Additional Resources"
  9. Or, refer user to source location, “case.edu/registrar/faculty/sisguides”
  10. “Missing Grades Report”
  11. Report a list of courses in an academic organization (department) or school/college that have not submitted final grades for a given term or series of terms.
  12. The report lists the following data for each course with outstanding grades:
    • Subject code
    • Catalog number
    • Section number
    • Class number
    • Enrollment total
    • Grades missing from enrollment total
    • Grades missing from grade roster
    • Roster status
    • Instructor name
    • Instructor email
  13. Resolve the ticket.
