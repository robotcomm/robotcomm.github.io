Early account closure; Account closure; Network account; Account suspension; Information security; Acceptable Use Policy (AUP); AUP violation

Service Description

When a person leaves the university, their network accounts are terminated or suspended in accordance with Case Network Account Closure Policy. If certain conditions are met, network privileges should be suspended more quickly than provided for in the standard procedure. Examples of these conditions include:

  • Employee termination presents an imminent threat to information or IT assets
  • Student suspended via judicial process and grace period is no longer applicable
  • Departing employee is a system administrator with elevated privileges
  • Any account holder is violates the university’s Acceptable Use Policy

The referenced procedure coordinates actions between CWRU IT Services and the Department of Human Resources. It is governed by the Case Network Account Closure Policy.

Use this article when:

  1. A supervisor or manager has question or about restricting an employee, student or affiliate access to his or her CWRU Network Account,
  2. A representative of the Department of Human Resources has a question about ITS procedure with regard to early account closure.

Tools for Resolution

Required Information

  • Name,
  • CWRU Network ID,
  • Contact information (preferred and alternate),
  • Problem or Request description

Resolver Groups

Network Services Engineering, Information Security

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

  1. Ed Rynes, edward.rynes@case.edu, (216) 368-3064 (office)

Escalation procedures

Escalation is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Escalations are are not available during or unplanned university closures, or during recognized university holidays.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

How to handle requests for immediate network account termination

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Supervisors may request immediate termination of an employee’s network account for emergency cases by contacting the Office of Employee Relations in the Department of Human Resources.
  3. Resolve the ticket or forward to Network Services Engineering resolver group.

Who reviews and approves requests for immediate network account termination

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Employee (faculty or staff): Department of Human Resources will review the request for validity and applicability.
  3. If approved, a representatives from Employee Relations will send an email request to the address: account-closure-hr@case.edu
  4. Student, alumni, or affiliate account: Information Security Office will review the request with the appropriate university governing organization.
  5. Network Services Engineering will only process early account termination requests from the Department of Human Resources or the Information Security Office.
  6. If request is denied, standard account termination processes apply.
  7. Resolve the ticket.

How early network account terminations are performed

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Network Services Engineering will complete any necessary system changes and respond to the Department of Human Resources or the Information Security Office within 24 hours (or within an agreed upon timeframe).
  3. Resolve the ticket.

Non-infrastructure accounts (e.g., vendor subscriptions, share drive accounts, etc.)

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Termination of access to IT systems that are managed outside of Case Directory Accounts is not within the scope of network account termination under early or standard account closure procedures.
  3. Resolve the ticket or forward to Information Security resolver group.
