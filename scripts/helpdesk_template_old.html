INFORMATION FOR TECHNICAL WRITER
Leading information (generally) should not exceed 2 brief paragraphs. Separate logically related information with subheadings. Use an unordered (bullet) list to simplify complex ideas. Use Markdown to generate consistently formatted, lightweight and portable documents. Syntax and guidelines follow.

  • Start with a Markdown primer @ Daring Fireball. Or, simply google ‘Markdown sytax’.
  • We use the git-hub flavor of Markdown. Fancypants is not implemented for Footprints.
  • Section headings are Heading 2 CTRL+2.
  • Subheads within a section (e.g., ‘Use this article when’, ‘Additional Resources’, procedural questions) are Heading 4 CTRL+4
  • NOTES begin with BOLD **NOTE:** and are offset in a > BLOCKQUOTE even with the left margin. Place notes at the end of a numbered list, when used.
  • Use * unordered list items when appropriate but avoid nested lists when possible.
  • Always use 1. ordered lists for procedural steps, index items and any other instance that explicitly or implicitly requires an analyst to make a judgment (e.g., ‘Use this article when’.)
  • Avoid using ** italicized text except to distinguish user groups in the Service Description.
  • Footprints does NOT support codeblocks (4 spaces preceding TEXT). Do not use.

Escalation

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group.
