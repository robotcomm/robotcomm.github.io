Service Description

Unified voice messaging at Case Western Reserve provides users with two paths to manage voicemail messages; a web-based interface and access via telephone. Users are able to,

  • Record voice messages using a computer
  • Set personal preferences for message playback (e.g., speed, order, etc.)
  • Set or change a voicemail passcode (PIN)
  • Record and set voicemail greetings
  • Create private lists

Web Inbox, also found at um.case.edu, allows users to

  • Play, review, reply to, compose, address and send voicemail messages in an ‘email-like’ environment
  • Delete messages (syncs with phone)

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation procedures

Escalations are available whenever requested, including during planned university closures and recognized university holidays.

During unplanned university closures escalations may NOT be available.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

How do I set or change my voicemail PIN

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Determine whether user is calling about a shared mailbox or in individual mailbox.
  3. If shared mailbox, STOP. These instructions are for individual mailboxes ONLY. Return to knowledgebase and search for appropriate instructions.
  4. The terms phone password, PIN and passcode are used interchangeably.

  5. Refer to the steps below.

    NOTE: User may also set a PIN from an internet-connected computer.

  6. Resolve ticket.

    Using your desk phone

  7. Press messages button on telephone.
  8. While listening to the main menu, press 5, 3, 1.
  9. The default PIN (passcode) is 10900.

  10. Enter a new 5-digit PIN and press * to confirm it.

    From another phone

  11. Call your telephone number (e.g., 216-368-1111).
  12. Press * to skip your greeting.
  13. While listening to the main menu, press 5, 3, 1.
  14. Enter a new 5-digit PIN and press * to confirm it.

Access Unity Connection

NOTE: The default passcode (PIN) is 10900

  1. Call Cisco Unity Connection.
  2. From your desk phone:
    1. Dial your phone number OR press the Messages button on your phone
  3. From outside your organization:
    1. Dial your phone number
    2. Press * when Cisco Unity Connection answers
  4. If prompted, enter your Connection ID (desk phone extension) and press #
  5. Enter your passcode (PIN) and press #
