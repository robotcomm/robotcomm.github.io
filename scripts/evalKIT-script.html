Service Description

EvaluationKIT is the current vendor-provided service for completing course evaluation surveys.

Tools for Resolution

Required Information

  • Name
  • CWRU Network ID
  • Contact information (preferred and alternate)
  • Course(s) affected
  • Problem or Request description

Resolver Groups

Course Evaluations: Available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Inquiries placed before 5 p.m. will receive a same-day response. Inquiries placed outside of operating hours will receive a response on the next business day.

How to Escalate

Issue escalation is not offered at this time. All unresolved tickets should be assigned to the Course Evaluations resolver group.

Troubleshoot + Repair

Complete course evaluations

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Students enrolled in a participating course receive an email notification with instructions and a link to EvaluationKIT.
  3. Students may be required to provide credentials at the SSO page, if not already signed in.
  4. No additional credentials are required to use EvaluationKIT.
  5. Once student has accessed EvaluationKIT dashboard, available surveys are shown in a list.
  6. Survey open and due dates, and submission status are also shown.
  7. User role (e.g., student, instructor, administrator) is shown in the upper-right corner of the screen.
  8. Click course name or number to begin course evaluation.
  9. Choose “Submit” to finish evaluation, “Come Back Later” to save progress and exit, or “Exit” to abandon survey answers.
  10. Instructors taking courses as a student
  11. Click user role to reveal additional options.
  12. Choose “Student/Respondent” option to reveal course evaluation surveys.
  13. Click course name or number to begin course evaluation.
  14. Choose “Submit” to finish evaluation, “Come Back Later” to save progress and exit, or “Exit” to abandon survey answers.
  15. Resolve the ticket

NOTE: Users who choose to access via mobile application (install from Google Play or iOS app store) follow the same steps to use EvaluationKIT.

How-to + Training

Departmental policies

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. A user must be defined as an instructor in EvaluationKIT before he or she may add survey questions.
  3. A department may determine whether survey questions should be added to course evaluations, and by whom.
  4. ITS does not make or enforce policies that specify who may be defined as an instructor within EvaluationKIT.

  5. EvaluationKIT does not provide a method to set individual permissions.

    NOTE: If an instructor accidentally modifies a survey in a way prohibited by his or her department, the instructor should contact a school/department EvaluationKIT administrator to restore the survey to its original settings.

  6. ITS requires all course evaluation surveys are finalized 1 week (7 calendar days) prior to the date of survey open.
  7. All available survey questions are pre-approved by FSCUE.
  8. Resolve the ticket.

Add questions

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Create custom question survey
    1. From dashboard, find name of project in "Custom Questions" section.
    2. Verify that Status is "Open" and Delivery Date is no fewer than seven (7) calendar days in the future.
    3. Click the name of the project to access it.
    4. Click View Main Survey for this Project to review previously selected survey questions.
    5. Click X to return to the previous screen.
    6. Click + Create New Survey.
    7. Add a survey title and brief description as instructed by area or departmental guidelines.
    8. Click Save New.
    9. Choose "Item Bank Questions" from the drop down menu.
    10. Choose the Item Bank survey name and click get questions.
    11. Choose to "select all" or add only specific questions.
    12. After selections are complete, click add selected.
    13. Click exit to return to previous page.
  3. Attach survey to project
    1. Click + Add Custom Question Survey.
    2. Choose name of newly created survey from list shown.
    3. User will be prompted to select courses.
    4. Choose appropriate course(s), section(s).
    5. Click Select Courses to complete process.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

Duplicated questions

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.

  2. If a duplicate question is added to a course evaluation survey by an instructor or designee, that person must also remove it.

    NOTE: Administrators at dept or college level CANNOT remove instances of duplicate questions added by an Instructor to a course.

    Because EvaluationKIT does not provide a method to prevent a question from being added to a survey more than once, it is important that instructors and administrative personnel coordinate to avoid duplication.

  3. ITS does not monitor the content of course evaluation surveys for erroneous duplicates.
  4. ITS requires all course evaluation surveys are finalized 1 week (7 calendar days) prior to the date of survey open.
  5. Resolve the ticket.

Survey results

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Instructors will receive an email when course evaluation results are available.
  3. Email contains a link to access results.
  4. If an instructor wishes to regenerate results, he or she should sign in to EvaluationKIT using the link provided for results.
    From the EvaluationKIT dashboard, choose Results (in the upper-right corner) and then choose from the following,
    • Response Rate Tracker - track course evaluation response rate. Choose a semester (project), and then search for course or section using course code or course title if needed.
    • Project Results - Search for semester (project). Click icon in View column to open results. Choose an individual course and click the icon in Report column OR select several courses (or sections) and click “Batch Report”.
    • Instructor Results - results for individual instructors. Results are pre-determined based on current user credentials.
    • Report Builder - build custom reports. ITS strongly recommends administrator guidance if using this option
      1. Click Create New Report
      2. Add report name and brief description. Click Continue.
      3. Search for and add Projects. Click Continue.
      4. Choose survey questions to add to report. Click Continue.
      5. Choose report “area” (e.g., respondent location in hierarchy - ex., limit to respondents from CAS Mathematics dept.)
      6. Choose query parameters (e.g., question #1 where responses are selected and the response is greater than x_value)
      7. Click Save and then Continue.
      8. Choose a report template. Previews for each option are shown.
      9. Click Generate.
      10. Results are shown. NOTE: If no results match query parameters, No Data Found is displayed in red.
    • Results Feedback - review survey comments
  5. Resolve the ticket or assign to Course Evaluations resolver group.

FAQ - students

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Refer to the shaded box below.
  3. Resolve the ticket.
How do I access my online course evaluations
Students receive an email notification with a link to course evaluations when they are ready.

Why don’t I see all of my courses when I log in?
Not all courses are evaluated using EvaluationKIT. Speak with an instructor for additional information.

Will my answers be lost if I stop in the middle of the evaluation?
Students have the option to complete course evaluation responses in more than one session. Choose “Come Back Later” to save progress and exit.

Will evaluation results be available to faculty before grades are posted?
No. Reports will not be available until after final grades are due.

How can I tell if I have completed all of my evaluations?
EvaluationKIT displays response status in the survey dashboard.

Why should I complete my online course evaluations?
Student feedback is vital to identifying strengths and weaknesses of courses, programs, and faculty instruction.

Are course evaluations mandatory?
Course evaluations are not mandatory. However, student feedback is vital to identifying strengths and weaknesses of courses, programs, and faculty instruction.

How are the results of course evaluations used?
Faculty are encouraged to use the results in evaluating their own teaching and planning future courses.

Who do I contact if I have more questions about the process or if I have difficulty accessing EvaluationKit?
Speak with your instructor to get help with the course evaluation process. Call (216) 368-HELP if you are having difficulty accessing your course evaluation.

FAQ - instructors

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Refer to the shaded box below.
  3. Resolve the ticket.
What are the benefits to transitioning to an online course evaluation system?
  • More thoughtful and meaningful student responses
  • Instruction time is not sacrificed when for course evaluations
  • Instructors receive results (summary reports, student comments) much more quickly
Can instructors ask additional questions on the evaluations?
An instructor may add additional questions from the FSCUE pre-approved question bank at the discretion of his or her department or as provided by his or her departmental policy. However, FSCUE findings suggest that response rates are greatest for course evaluation surveys with no more than five (5) questions.

Will I be able to monitor response rates while the course evaluation is open for students?
Yes. Instructors may monitor their response rates for each class using the Response Rate Tracker.

How will I know when my evaluation results are available?
Instructors will receive an email indicating that the results are available and where to retrieve them.

How do I save a copy of my course evaluation results?
When they become available, results may be downloaded as a PDF or Excel file.

Can I see my results from past semesters?
Results from course evaluations are available in EvaluationKIT beginning in spring 2015.

Who will have access to the results of my course evaluations?
The results are available to you, your department head and the college Dean.

What if I am an instructor taking courses?
Click “user role” at upper-right to reveal additional options. Choose “Student/Respondent” option to reveal course evaluation surveys. Follow on-screen instructions to complete course evaluation survey.

Who do I contact if I have more questions about the process or if I have difficulty accessing EvaluationKit?
Speak with your school EvaluationKIT administrator or call (216) 368-HELP if you are having difficulty accessing course evaluations/results.
