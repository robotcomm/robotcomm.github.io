Service Description

Audio Visual services and equipment at the Law School (e.g., SmartBoards, projectors, conference room phones, telepresence units, etc.) are wholly operated and maintained by Law School IT.

Analysts may address questions concerning these services using the standard AV / TEC scripts. However, all issues that are not resolved by the analyst should be assigned to the Law School AV resolver group for further investigation.

NOTE: DO NOT assign any ticket to MediaVision UNLESS the the incident is symptomatic of a problem with a ‘university-level’ MediaVision service (e.g., Echo360).

Use this article when:

  1. Any student, instructor or staff person has a general question about AV services at the law school (e.g., reserving or operating equipment, services available in a particular room, billing / charges, etc.),
  2. Any student, instructor or staff person has a specific issue or problem while operating equipment at the law school,
  3. Any student, instructor or staff person needs to report an service outage, broken / damaged equipment or needs to escalate an issue to Law School IT Administration.

NOTE: Provide the user the followup email address “its-cluster-admin@case.edu” before resolving any ticket.

Additional Resources

Please refer to existing general-purpose and TEC scripts for troubleshooting steps. All unresolved issues that are not escalated and law school specific should be assigned to the Law School AV resolver group.

Resolver Groups

Law School AV, Law School IT Administration, MediaVision (global issues only)

Law School AV is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST (and evenings as scheduled).

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

All escalations should be marked as such and assigned to the Law School IT Administration resolver group.

Escalation procedures

Audio Visual services are not considered critical.

Escalation is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Escalations are are not available during or unplanned university closures, or during recognized university holidays.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.
