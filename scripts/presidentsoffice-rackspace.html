Exchange, Hosted @ Rackspace
VIP Support

ITS supports hosted Exchange service used by the President's office. Any ticket (except an outage) that cannot be resolved by an Analyst should be assigned to Deskside (DSS).

Escalate all outage reports as described below. A Service Desk Analyst should never contact the vendor or open a vendor support ticket for any reason.

Important All callers should be reminded that as a platinum customer, he or she is entitled to request in-person service at any time.

Tools for Resolution

Required Information

  • Name
  • Contact information (preferred and alternate)
  • Case Network ID
  • Location
  • Problem or Request description

Resolver Groups

Deskside Support (DSS) is available from 9 AM-5 PM, M-F.

How to Escalate

Exchange (hosted at Rackspace) is not considered a critical service. However, because this service is only offered to VIP customers, all incidents receive high-priority treatment.

Escalations are available whenever requested, including during planned university closures and recognized university holidays.

During unplanned university closures escalations may NOT be available.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

Escalation not available.

Activate + Setup + Install

Install Guidebook

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. iOS, Android, Blackerry 10+: Use ANY of the following methods to download and install Guidebook. Follow on-screen prompts to complete.
    • Scan QR code provided with orientation materials.
    • Navigate to guidebook.com/getit on your device's web browser.
    • Go to your device app store and search for "Guidebook".
  3. Desktop, Windows Phone, non-Blackberry 10+, other: Navigate to guidebook.com and browse using your web browser.
  4. If user is still having problems, direct to firstyearexperience@case.edu.
  5. Resolve ticket.

How-to + Training

Find a Guide, iOS

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Open the Guidebook app on your device.
  3. Use the Search box at the top of the screen to search for your guide by name ("Case Western Reserve University Orientation 2015", use partial for search).
  4. Choose desired guide from the results and tap "Get this Guide" to download it.
  5. Resolve ticket.

Find a Guide, Android

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Open the Guidebook app on your device.
  3. Use the Search box at the top of the screen to search for your guide by name ("Case Western Reserve University Orientation 2015", use partial for search).
  4. Choose desired guide from the results and tap "Get this Guide" to download it.
  5. If your event organizer has provided a QR code to scan the guide, you can select the scan option. Center your camera on the QR code icon at the end of the Search bar, and it will automatically scan the code and take you to the guide.
  6. Resolve ticket.

Find a Guide, Other

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Go to guidebook.com/browse/ on your device's internet browser.
  3. Click the search bar below "Select a Category".
  4. Use the Search box at the top of the screen to search for your guide by name ("Case Western Reserve University Orientation 2015", use partial for search).
  5. Choose desired guide from the results and tap its name to download it.
  6. Resolve ticket.

General Navigation

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. For iOS devices: Swipe the screen from right to left to access additional pages of icons and content. If there are no more pages, swiping will have no effect.
  3. For Android: Tap the icon (three lines) in the top left of your screen to access the content drawer. Scroll down to see all guide icons and content.
  4. For devices that have a hardware "search" button (typically a magnifying glass icon): Tap this button while in the app to perform a search.
  5. For devices lacking a "search" button, but containing a "Menu" button: Tap the "Menu" icon to load the contextual menu and then select "Search Guide" to perform a search.
  6. For devices with neither of the above: Tap the contextual menu button on the top right-hand side of the screen (looks like three squares stacked vertically). Then tap "Search Guide".
  7. All other devices: Scroll down to view the icons and content within the guide. Use the web browser's built-in back button to navigate back within a guide, and use the built-in "Find" function to search for any information on page.
  8. Resolve ticket.

Schedule and Schedule Tracks

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. The application home screen contains both the full program schedules (i.e., Parent and Family Orientation) and individual schedule tracks (i.e., Parents and Families of Mistletoe).
  3. Sessions may be color coded to denote specific tracks and audiences for the sessions.
  4. A schedule track functions identically to the full schedule.
  5. The track exists as a segmented version of the larger schedule, tailored specifically to a particular sub-group of the orientation.
  6. CWRU recommends that users refer to both for the best experience.
  7. Tap the schedule name to view it.
  8. Resolve ticket.

My Schedule

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Create a personalized schedule, with reminders, from any of the existing sessions in the application.
  3. To add an item to your schedule:
    • Open any schedule in the app.
    • iOS: Swipe a session to add the session to your personalized schedule.
    • Android: Tap and hold a session to add the session to your personalized schedule.
  4. Alternatively, open any session and navigate to the bottom of your screen. Click "Add to My Schedule".
  5. Resolve ticket.

To-do List

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Create a personalized to-do list from any existing list items in your app or your own custom entries.
  3. To add an item to your todo list:
    • Open any list in the app.
    • iOS: Swipe an item to add it to your to-do list.
    • Android: Tap and hold an item to add it to your to-do list.
  4. Alternatively, you can tap into a list item and navigate to the bottom of your screen. Click "Add to To-Do".
  5. Tap an item to cross it off your to-do list.
  6. Resolve ticket.
