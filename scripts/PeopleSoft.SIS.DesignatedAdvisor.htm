Service Description

The designated advisor screen is intended to encourage academically-oriented discussions (performance, goals, mentoring, etc.) between students and professional educators or CWRU staff who do not have an established advising relationship.

Key benefits for the student include,

  • Broad, cross-discipline pool of advising candidates
  • Institutional facilitated networking opportunities
  • No applications or paperwork to enroll
  • Flexible, informal mentoring relationships are fit to student interests within and without selected field of study

Use this article when:

  1. Any student user requests additional information regarding the Designated Advisor program,
  2. Any student user needs assistance navigating or completing a task within the Designated Advisor SIS screens.
  3. Any educator, researcher or staff member requests additional information regarding the Designated Advisor program, including requests to be added to the advisor pool.

NOTE: Non-functional questions that are not covered in this script will always be forwarded to the Peoplesoft SIS-Reg resolver group.

Detailed Troubleshoot / Repair Procedures

return to top | index | troubleshoot questions

What is the ‘advisor box’? Is this new?

return to troubleshoot questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘The advisor box appears in the lower-right section of the Student Center when a student signs in to SIS, below enrollment dates.’
  3. Advise user, ‘Your faculty advisor and other designated advisor(s), when selected, display in this box. If you haven’t yet selected a designated advisor(s), only your faculty (academic) advisor name will display.’
  4. Advise user, ‘To view additional information about any advisor(s), or to add or remove a designated advisor(s), click the details link in the lower right corner.
  5. Resolve the ticket.

I want my designated advisor to release a hold. Why isn’t it working?

return to troubleshoot questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘Designated advisors have access to view academic and personal information stored in SIS. However, a designated advisor may not release any, regardless of their role at the university.’
  3. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Refer any user with objections or who requests additional clarification to the Registrar’s office.

I asked educator/staff member to be a designated mentor. Why can’t he or she add information to my student record?

return to troubleshoot questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘A student may add (or remove) a designated advisor(s) from his or her student record in SIS. However, a potential designated advisor has no access to view or modify a student record.’
  3. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Refer any user with objections or who requests additional clarification to the Registrar’s office.

I am unable to sign in to SIS to add/change/remove a designated advisor(s).

return to troubleshoot questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Verify that the user is a current CWRU student.
    Use the ID Lookup Tool to determine whether the user has a working CWRU ID and Password.
  3. If the student user has an active, working CWRU ID and Password and cannot access SIS forward ticket to Peoplesoft SIS-Reg resolver group.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Only student users with an active, working CWRU ID and Password will have access to update a designated advisor(s) through SIS. Additional information regarding this topic may be found in the Identity Management scripts.

Detailed How To / Training Procedures

return to top | index | training questions

What is a ‘designated advisor’ and how does that person differ from an academic advisor?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘A designated advisor is a professional educator, researcher or CWRU staff member to whom a student opens his or her academic and personal records. Unlike a faculty advisor, the relationship between designated advisor and student is informal, voluntary, student-initiated and may be oriented to academic, professional or avocational interests outside of a declared field of study.’
  3. Recap the program overview and benefits to the student.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Refer requests for additional information to the Registrar’s office.

Am I required to designate an advisor(s)?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘The Registrar’s Office recommends that all students consider the benefits of electing one or more designated advisors. Remember, there is no cost or paperwork needed to participate. Also, students may change or remove a designated advisor at any time.’
  3. Recap the program overview and benefits to the student.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Refer requests for additional information to the Registrar’s office.

May I designate more than one advisor?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Advise user, ‘SIS supports multiple selected ‘designated advisors’. You may wish to discuss the strategies for effective participation with a representative from the Registrar’s office or with your academic advisor before making a selection.’
  3. Resolve the ticket.

NOTE: Refer requests for additional information to the Registrar’s office.

What are the steps to add a designated advisor(s)?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Sign in to SIS with your CWRU Network ID and Password.
  3. Find the Advisor box in the lower-right section of the Student Center screen.
  4. Click the green arrow in the title bar of the box to expand it.
  5. Click the details link.
  6. On the subsequent screen, select the tab labeled designated advisors.
  7. Search for an advisor by his or her CWRU Network ID. Click Add Designated Advisor ID.
  8. Confirm that selected ID matches desired advisor and click Confirm & Save Designated Advisor.
  9. Resolve the ticket or forward to Peoplesoft SIS-Reg resolver group.

How do I change or remove a previously designated advisor?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Sign in to SIS with your CWRU Network ID and Password.
  3. Find the Advisor box in the lower-right section of the Student Center screen.
  4. Click the green arrow in the title bar of the box to expand it.
  5. Click the details link.
  6. On the subsequent screen, select the tab labeled designated advisors.
  7. Click the Remove button to delete an existing designated advisor.
  8. Resolve the ticket or forward to Peoplesoft SIS-Reg resolver group.

How do I access the designated advisor page?

return to training questions

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Sign in to SIS with your CWRU Network ID and Password.
  3. Find the Advisor box in the lower-right section of the Student Center screen.
  4. Click the green arrow in the title bar of the box to expand it.
  5. Click the details link.
  6. On the subsequent screen, select the tab labeled designated advisors.
  7. Resolve the ticket.
