Troubleshoot + Repair

Suspended printing privileges

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. User should access PaperCut using his or her CWRU Network ID and password.
  3. Print balance is displayed in center of the screen.
  4. PaperCut will allow a negative balance of as much as $5.00 (e.g., -5.00) before suspending printing privileges.
  5. Student should add funds to his or her account to activate printing privileges.
  6. Note all troubleshooting steps taken.
  7. Resolve the ticket.

No one can print

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Add instruction to ticket, “issue with one or both of the print servers - no one can print”.
  3. Assign the ticket to Windows Server and Law School IT Administration resolver groups.

Faculty or staff cannot print

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Add instruction to ticket, “restart Windows Firewall Service failed on lawprt2 - faculty and staff cannot print”.
  3. Assign the ticket to Windows Server and Law School IT Administration resolver groups.

Printer malfunction, error code, physical damage

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Verify printer brand.
  3. Note any error codes displayed.
  4. Troubleshoot common print issues, as appropriate (e.g., toner, paper jam, powered on and connected, etc.)
  5. Probe for additional possible causes of issue (e.g., Google Cloud Print, drivers, connectivity, etc.)
  6. Note all troubleshooting steps taken.
  7. Resolve the ticket OR
  8. If Kyocera then assign ticket to Meritech resolver group.
  9. Otherwise, assign ticket to DSS.

    NOTE: If the user is a law school employee, provide vendor contact information

    Printer brand Vendor name Contact number
    Kyocera Meritech (216) 741-3222
    All others ALT (216) 663-8181

Out of paper

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. For lab managed printers, instruct user to refer to sticker on printer for paper contact.
  3. For all others, direct the user to contact the circulation desk.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

Toner low

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Student users should contact the lab assistant.
  3. If a lab assistant is not available, the student should contact circulation.
  4. Toner for student printers is kept in stock.
  5. User is a law school employee
  6. Verify printer brand.
  7. Instruct user to contact vendor directly (see table below) and provide printer number from vendor's sticker on the side of the printer.
  8. Resolve the ticket.

    NOTE: If the user is a law school employee, provide vendor contact information

    Printer brand Vendor name Contact number
    Kyocera Meritech (216) 741-3222
    All others ALT (216) 663-8181

Job did not print

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Student has access to multiple printing accounts (e.g. personal and Law Review).
  3. Submit the print job to cloud print.
  4. Go to https://lawprt1.case.edu:9192/client.
  5. Sign in CWRU network ID and password.
  6. Choose appropriate account to charge.
  7. “ReleaseLater” printers require the student release the print job from a release station computer (by the printers in the 3rd floor lab or outside Law Review) or the PaperCut website.
    1. Sign in to PaperCut with CWRU network ID and password
    2. Click Jobs Pending Release
    3. Choose job to release and click Print.
  8. Turn the printer off and back on.
  9. Resolve the ticket or assign to Law School IT Administration resolver group.

    NOTE: Google Cloud Print does not handle delayed print jobs well. If possible, use “ReleaseNow” printers with Google Cloud Print.

Law school printers are not shown

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Verify that the user is signed in to Google services using his or her CWRU network ID and password (e.g., university account).
  3. If using a browser to access the service, use Chrome.
  4. Resolve the ticket or assign to Law School IT Administration resolver group.

Law school printers are shown - all offline / greyed out

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Service is temporarily unavailable.
  3. Add instruction to ticket, “restart lawprt1”.
  4. Assign the ticket to Windows Server and Law School IT Administration resolver groups.

Law school printers are shown - some offline / greyed out

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Google may have put printer in “dormant” status.
  3. Attempt to send a job to the printer to cause it to “wake up”.
  4. Add instruction to ticket, “Cloud print - printer unresponsive. Unpublish printerName and re-publish it”.
  5. Include student information in ticket if a refund is requested.
  6. Resolve the ticket or assign Law School IT Administration resolver group.

Activate + Setup + Install

Wireless printing (PaperCut)

back to top

The law school provides a service called PaperCut Web Print which allows users to print wirelessly, without installing drivers.

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Sign in to PaperCut with CWRU network ID and password.
  3. Click Web Print (left menu).
  4. Click Submit a Job.
  5. Choose a printer.
  6. Click Print Options and Account Selections.
  7. Choose the number of copies.
  8. Choose an account to charge.
  9. Click Upload Document.
  10. Click Browse to locate file to print.
  11. Click Upload & Complete.
  12. Click Held in Queue and then Print to release print job.
  13. Resolve the ticket

Use Google Cloud Print

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Walk user through steps for appropriate application(s) / platform(s) as shown below.
  3. Refer user to additional information provided by Law School IT Administration.
  4. Resolve the ticket.

    NOTE: Google Cloud Print does not handle delayed print jobs well. If possible, use “ReleaseNow” printers with Google Cloud Print.

Google Drive for Mac, PC, or Chromebook
This option is built into Google Chrome. Functionality in other browsers is not guaranteed.

  1. Download Google Chrome.
  2. Go to webmail and log in with your CWRU network ID and password.
  3. Click apps menu in the upper-right and choose Drive.
  4. Open the document you would like to print.
  5. Click the Google Drive print button OR File > Print.
  6. Click Change… Destination.
  7. Under “Google Cloud Print” choose preferred printer.
  8. If the Law School printers are not shown, make sure that your University email address is selected in the “Show destinations for” drop down menu in the upper right.
  9. Choose a “ReleaseNow” printer to print immediately.

    NOTE: If you have access to more than your personal printing account (e.g. Law Review, JIL, etc.), open a browser tab, go to https://lawprt1.case.edu:9192/client, log in with your University username and password, select the account to charge, and click “Print”.

Google Cloud Printer for PC
Google offers a solution for Windows PCs that installs a “Google Cloud Printer” as a standard printer available in any application that supports printing.

  1. Using Google Chrome, download and install Google Cloud Printer
  2. In any application that supports printing, go to File > Print.
  3. Choose “Google Cloud Printer” and click OK.
  4. Enter your CWRU email address (username@case.edu) and click “Sign in”.
  5. Login to CWRU Single Sign-On.
  6. Choose printer options.
  7. Click Print.
  8. Choose a “ReleaseNow” printer to print immediately.

    NOTE: If you have access to more than your personal printing account (e.g. Law Review, JIL, etc.), open a browser tab, go to https://lawprt1.case.edu:9192/client, log in with your University username and password, select the account to charge, and click “Print”.

Google Drive for Android

  1. Install Cloud Print from the Play Store.
  2. Install **Google Drive from the Play Store. Google Docs, Google Sheets, Google Slides, and Chrome all support Cloud Print as well.
  3. Tap Drive icon to launch it.
  4. If CWRU account is not selected tap the three lines next to “My Drive” in the upper left to reveal a menu.
  5. Tap the currently selected account to open an option to select or add your CWRU account.
  6. Tap the “i” button next to the document to get more information.
  7. Tap the ellipsis (…) button and then Print.
  8. Choose printer and options.
  9. Tap Print.

  10. Choose a “ReleaseNow” printer to print immediately.

    NOTE: If you have access to more than your personal printing account (e.g. Law Review, JIL, etc.), open a browser tab, go to https://lawprt1.case.edu:9192/client, log in with your University username and password, select the account to charge, and click “Print”.

Google Drive for iOS (iPhone, iPad)

  1. Install **Google Drive from the App Store. Google Docs, Google Sheets, Google Slides, and Chrome all support Cloud Print as well.
  2. Tap Drive icon to launch it.
  3. If CWRU account is not selected tap the three lines next to “My Drive” in the upper left to reveal a menu.
  4. Tap the currently selected account to open an option to select or add your CWRU account.
  5. Tap the “i” button next to the document to get more information.
  6. Tap Print and then Google Cloud Print.
  7. Tap Print.

  8. Choose a “ReleaseNow” printer to print immediately.

    NOTE: If you have access to more than your personal printing account (e.g. Law Review, JIL, etc.), open a browser tab, go to https://lawprt1.case.edu:9192/client, log in with your University username and password, select the account to charge, and click “Print”.
