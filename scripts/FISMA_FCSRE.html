Service Description

The FISMA Compliant Secure Research Environment (FCSRE) is a controlled environment and FISMA-based security program. Research data with sensitive content can be stored and analyzed inside the environment.

The Federal Information Security Act of 2002 (FISMA) is a law requiring protection of sensitive data created, stored or accessed by the Federal Government or any entity on its behalf.

ITS is currently preparing space in a physically secure commercial data center with vendor partners. The FCSRE is likely to be completely operational in March 2015.

How to Escalate

Who to contact for escalation

FISMA Compliant Secure Research Environment planning is not considered a critical service. Issues that may in other cases be escalated should be assigned to the Research Computing resolver group.

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation procedures

Escalation is not available at this time. Assign all unresolved issues to the Research Computing resolver group.

    FCSRE is important to CWRU for tactical and strategic reasons.

    Tactical
    Data containing personal identifiers, including clinical data from affiliated hospitals, is used in research by CWRU faculty, instructors and students. This data needs to be protected as required in federal and state regulations.

    Strategic
    A fully-FISMA compliant research environment provides competitive advantage to CWRU researchers seeking funding through grants and contracts.

    The FCSRE will be used to store, manage and analyze data containing personal health information (PHI).

    It will include secured systems for data storage and management (e.g., LabMatrix, REDCap, OnCore and APEX) as well as software for analyzing data (e.g., SAS, SPSS and R).

    FCSRE software will be accessed by users via virtual desktop technology.
