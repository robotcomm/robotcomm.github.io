Service Description

Select network nodes, services and appliances are managed wholly by the Case School of Engineering, CSE IT. All issues regarding CSE IT-managed services that are not resolved by the analyst should be assigned to the CSE IT resolver group for further investigation.


NOTE: Faceplate installations, faceplate repairs, firewall issues, fiber infrastructure, purpose-built network spaces (e.g., Science DMZ), general internet access (wired and wireless), access to Internet2 and other enumerated network services are provided by central ITS.

Issues and questions concerning any ITS service should be directed to a named ITS resolver group, per script-specific instructions.

Use this article when:

  1. Any student, instructor or staff person has a general question about network elements managed by the Case School of Engineering,
  2. Any student, instructor or staff person has a specific issue or problem with a network element managed by the Case School of Engineering,
  3. Any student, instructor or staff person needs to report an outage or needs to escalate an issue to CSE IT.

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

All escalations should be marked as such and assigned to the CSE IT resolver group.

Escalation procedures

Network support at the Case School of Engineering is not considered critical, except when a failure may impact essential research or job functions within the school.

Escalation is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Escalations are not available during planned or unplanned university closures, or during recognized university holidays.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

Outage

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Is the outage symptomatic of problems with Case School of Engineering managed network elements?
  3. If during available hours, warm transfer caller to CSE IT.
  4. Assign ticket to CSE IT resolver group.
  5. Is the outage symptomatic of problems with ITS managed network elements?
  6. Refer to appropriate script and follow assignment or escalation procedures shown there.

Unable to Resolve

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Note user’s availability, if given.
  3. Note user’s preferred contact method (e.g., cell, office phone, email), if given.
  4. Note any troubleshooting steps taken, and the result.
  5. Assign to CSE IT resolver group.
