Service Description

Server operations, equipment and room access at the Case School of Engineering (e.g., Grove, Nashua, Bern, Chicago, etc.) are wholly the responsibility of CSE IT.

Analysts may address questions concerning these services using the standard server / connectivity scripts. However, all issues that are not resolved by the analyst should be assigned to the CSE IT resolver group for further investigation.

NOTE: DO NOT assign any ticket to any ITS resolver group UNLESS the the incident is symptomatic of a problem with a ‘university-level’ service OR the ticket submitter is a member of CSE IT.

Use this article when:

  1. Any student, instructor or staff person has a general question about server or storage services managed by the Case School of Engineering (including requests to access server rooms),
  2. Any student, instructor or staff person has a specific issue or problem while operating equipment or attempting to access resources located on servers managed by the Case School of Engineering,
  3. Any student, instructor or staff person needs to report a service outage, broken / damaged equipment or needs to escalate an issue to CSE IT.

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

All escalations should be marked as such and assigned to the CSE IT resolver group.

Escalation procedures

Server operations at the Case School of Engineering are not considered critical, except when a failure impacts essential research or job functions within the school.

Escalation is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Escalations are not available during planned or unplanned university closures, or during recognized university holidays.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

Outage

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Is the outage symptomatic of problems with Case School of Engineering maintained equipment / services?
  3. If during available hours, warm transfer caller to CSE IT.
  4. Assign ticket to CSE IT resolver group.
