How to escalate

Who to contact

  1. Telephone Services

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation procedures

Escalations are available whenever requested, including during planned university closures and recognized university holidays.

During unplanned university closures escalations may NOT be available.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

Troubleshoot + Repair

User needs shared mailbox ‘ID’

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Open the shared mailbox user spreadsheet provided by CWRU ITS.
  3. Locate the row where user’s name appears in the column labelled ‘name’.
  4. In the same row, locate the 7-digit number in the column labelled ‘personal mailbox number’.
  5. Shared mailbox users will provide this number whenever prompted for ‘ID’.
  6. Shared mailbox users will provide the 11-digit ‘shared mailbox number’ when prompted for ‘extension of the call handler’.
  7. The order of the mailbox users (e.g., press 1 for Jane Doe, press 2 for John Smith, etc.) is provided in the far right column. This is needed if a user is recording a standard greeting.
  8. Provide the information requested, explain its use, and probe for additional needs (for example, if the user is recording shared mailbox greeting, he or she will need each of the above pieces of information).
  9. Remind the user that the order and membership of shared mailboxes cannot be changed by end users. This must be completed by ITS (see, Add or remove shared mailbox members)
  10. Resolve ticket.

How-to + Training

What is a ‘shared mailbox’

back top

A shared mailbox is a single number shared among several voicemail users. A standard greeting provides instructions for callers who wish to leave a message for a member of the shared mailbox. However, this greeting is not provided by the system, and so must be recorded manually. Any member of the group may record a standard greeting for a shared mailbox.

Record a standard greeting

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Guide user through process
    1. Dial 368-8744 while on-campus or if off campus, dial 216-368-8744.
    2. When prompted for an ID enter your 7-digit personal mailbox number (ex., 888-2368) and press the pound (#) key.
    3. Enter your PIN (the default is 10900).
    4. When prompted for “extension of the call handler”, enter your 11-digit shared mailbox number (ex., +1-216-368-1234) and press the pound (#) key.
    5. Press 2 when prompted to access settings. Then, press 2 again to record a standard greeting.
    6. Follow audio prompts to review and save, or discard and re-record your greeting.
  3. Instructions for end users are available at case.edu/its/uc/special-instructions-for-shared-mailbox-users
  4. Resolve ticket.

Access messages

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Shared mailbox users access messages in almost exactly the same way as any other voicemail user.

  3. Users may access voicemail on- or off-campus from any telephone, or using the internet.

    Access messages online

    1. Type um.case.edu into your browser address bar.
    2. Sign in using your CWRU Network ID and password.
    3. Received messages are shown in reverse-chronological order.

    Access messages off-campus

    1. Dial +1-216-754-3800
    2. When prompted for your ID, enter 7-digit personal mailbox number (ex., 888-1234) and press the pound (#) key.
    3. Enter your PIN (default is 10900).
    4. Press the pound (#) key.

    Access messages on-campus

    1. Press the Messages key.
    2. When greeting begins, press the star (*) key.
    3. Enter your 7-digit personal mailbox number (ex., 888-1234)
    4. Press the pound (#) key.
    5. Enter your PIN (default is 10900).
    6. Press the pound (#) key.

    Access messages on-campus (analog VoIP phone)

    1. Press the star (*) key and then 0.
    2. Enter your PIN (default is 10900).
    3. Press the pound (#) key.

  4. Instructions for end users are available at case.edu/its/uc/how-to-access-voicemail

  5. Resolve ticket.
