Service Description

CWRU offers digital cable television (CATV) to all residential and Greek students. Service is provided by Campus Televideo and Time Warner Cable.

In order to receive cable television services, you must have a digital QAM tuner. Digital tuners have been required in televisions since 2007, but not all televisions included the QAM tuner. Converter boxes that can tune digital cable TV signals are available for sale at many retail outlets.

Use this article when

  1. A student needs help connecting CATV at a location maintained by Student Affairs IT.
  2. Any user reports a partial or total CATV outage.

How to Escalate

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Who to contact for escalation

  1. Student Affairs IT, (216) 368-1959 (office)
  2. Joel Kraft, (216) 687-6489 (mobile)

Escalation procedures

Although cable television is not considered a critical service, global outages are treated as critical events.

Classify the reason for escalation as one of the following; Confirmed Outage (total or partial), Suspected Outage (total or partial), Abend, Slow response time, User Requirements not met, Other functional or Other technical.

Event type Requirements
Critical outage one entire group of residence halls or more
Non-critical outage single channel outage, when there is also a major broadcast event
Other / Isolated events service interruption of one or more channels affecting single building / room / floor

Critical outages
Escalations are available whenever requested, including during planned university closures and recognized university holidays.

During unplanned university closures escalations may NOT be available.

Use published schedule and contact information to route P0 and P1 incidents to the appropriate resolver group and current Ops Manager On Call.

Non-critical outages
Escalation is available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Escalations are are not available during or unplanned university closures, or during recognized university holidays.

Other
Issue escalation is not offered. All unresolved tickets should be assigned to the Student Affairs IT or Faceplate resolver groups.

Detailed Troubleshoot / Repair Procedures

Troubleshooting tips from Student Affairs*

No television signal

return to index


  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Check cable TV service page, “Problems and Updates” section for current reported outages at user’s location.
  3. Check Footprints for related incidents of outage at user’s location.
  4. Coaxial cable should be connected firmly to both television and faceplate coaxial cable outlet, and at least finger tight at both ends.
  5. Is television compatible with CWRU cable network (e.g., contains QAM digital tuner)? See, How-To > Compatibility requirements.
  6. Complete a channel scan (this may require television user manual).
  7. If no signal still, connect the coaxial cable to another faceplate and complete a second channel scan.

    NOTE:

    Cable outlet may be either a wall plate (faceplate) or a cable coming out of the floor. There is usually one outlet per bedroom and common area.

    In a residence hall or Greek house, the cable outlet is located on a faceplate in each bedroom. In Clarke Tower and The Village at 115, there is also an outlet in each apartment common area. In property management apartments, the cable comes out of the floor or wall in bedrooms and some living rooms.

    There are two cable outlets in in each double room in the following residence halls (faceplates are located on opposite walls, and may be blocked by furniture. An outlet marked with a DO NOT USE sticker is still available to use for CATV);

    • Clarke Tower,
    • Cutler House,
    • Cutter House,
    • Hitchcock House,
    • Norton House,
    • Pierce House,
    • Raymond House,
    • Sherman House,
    • Storrs House,
    • Taplin House,
    • Tyler House.
  8. Note any observed damage to faceplate or coaxial connection in the ticket.
  9. If there is damage to the faceplate, but this is not the source of the problem, a second ticket should be opened detailing the damage and that ticket assigned to the Faceplate resolver group. NOTE THIS ACTION IN ORIGINAL TICKET.
  10. Note all troubleshooting steps taken in the ticket.
  11. Determine scope of outage or device compatibility issue (single user, multiple users, entire building, etc.)
  12. Resolve the ticket or forward to the MediaVision (academic or administrative buildings), Student Affairs IT (residence halls or Greek housing), or Faceplate (faceplate damage) resolver group.

Detailed Outage Procedures

User reports an outage

return to index

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Check cable TV service page, “Problems and Updates” section for current reported outages at user’s location.
  3. Check Footprints for related incidents of outage at user’s location.
  4. Troubleshoot coaxial connection, as appropriate.
  5. If new or changed equipment, verify compatibility with CWRU cable network.
  6. Troubleshoot faceplate issues, as appropriate.
  7. Note any troubleshooting steps taken.
  8. Note any observed damage to faceplate or coaxial connection in the ticket.
  9. If there is damage to the faceplate, but this is not the source of the problem, a second ticket should be opened detailing the damage and that ticket assigned to the Faceplate resolver group. NOTE THIS ACTION IN ORIGINAL TICKET.
  10. Determine scope of problem.
  11. Does the outage one channel only, or more than one channel?
  12. Does the outage prevent the user from watching a major broadcast event? If yes, what (e.g., sporting event, annual award show, significant political coverage or speech)?
  13. Is a neighbor or suitemate experiencing an outage?
  14. Is the common area or lobby television working?
  15. Note scope of problem.
  16. Provide escalation, if required by escalation procedures.
  17. Resolve the ticket, forward to the MediaVision (academic or administrative buildings) or forward to Student Affairs IT (residence halls or Greek housing).
