Service Description

CWRU Student Help and Resource Exchange (SHARE) is an application designed to promote undergraduate success. The application provides a common process for reporting behavioral incidents or patterns that may be indicative of future student failure.

Any faculty or staff person with valid Single-Sign On credentials may create a report and submit it CWRU SHARE.

Use this article when:

  1. Any faculty or staff person needs assistance,

    • accessing CWRU SHARE,
    • creating or submitting a report,
    • following up on a previously submitted report record

  2. Any faculty or staff person wishes to direct a comment to the application owner, request features or file a complaint.

  3. Any user needs to report an application outage

NOTE: CWRU SHARE is a Quickbase application. If for some reason Quickbase is not functioning, CWRU SHARE will also not function.

CWRU SHARE access is determined by LDAP roles and protected with Single-Sign On. A user with multiple affiliations whose primary affiliation ‘student’, or whose primary affiliation has recently changed may not be able to access CWRU SHARE.

Undergraduate students are specifically prohibited from accessing CWRU SHARE via Quickbase or associated database records through any means.

Tools for Resolution

Required Information

  • Name,
  • CWRU Network ID,
  • Contact information (preferred and alternate),
  • Problem or Request description

Resolver Groups

CWRU SHARE: Available Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Inquiries placed before 5 p.m. will receive a same-day response. Inquiries placed outside of operating hours will receive a response on the next business day.

Identity Management: Available as provided in issue-specific script to resolve Identity Management (network credentials, SSO, LDAP, etc.) issues.

How to Escalate

Who to contact for escalation

Issue escalation is not offered at this time. All unresolved tickets should be assigned to the CWRU SHARE resolver group.

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation procedures

CWRU SHARE is not considered a critical service. Issue escalation is not offered at this time.

Where can I find CWRU SHARE?

The Office of Undergraduate Studies hosts the public-facing interface for CWRU SHARE at their website, http://www.case.edu/ugstudies/.

The specific URL for the web form is: https://www.case.edu/ugstudies/cwrushare/index.html.


What is the purpose of CWRU SHARE?

CWRU SHARE promotes undergraduate success by providing a place for faculty and staff to voice their concerns about students who are, or may soon be in jeopardy.

There is no pre-set definition of an ‘early warning sign’ in this context. The Office of Undergraduate Studies relies on the experience of the CWRU community to promote healthy, productive behaviors conducive to the academic and personal growth of our students.

Report records added to CWRU SHARE are kept in strictest confidence, and considered a springboard for actionable outreach.


Who maintains CWRU SHARE?

The Office of Undergraduate Studies maintains CWRU SHARE. All report records entered into the database will be reviewed. Where appropriate, a staff member will followup with the individual who has submitted a report to discuss the concern and possible appropriate responses.


Who monitors CWRU SHARE?

Selected individuals within the Office of Undergraduate Studies receive immediate email notification when a report record has been entered into CWRU SHARE database.

Where appropriate, a staff member will follow up with the individual who has submitted a report record to discuss the concern and possible appropriate responses.

Complete a report

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Navigate to the form URL, https://www.case.edu/ugstudies/cwrushare/index.html.
  3. Enter CWRU credentials at Single-Sign On page, if prompted.

  4. Select or type in required information in the section titled “Your Information”. This includes;

    • Today’s date (pre-populated)
    • Name
    • CWRU ID
    • CWRU affiliation (choose faculty or staff)
    • School affiliation

  5. Select or type in required information in the section titled “Student’s Information.” This includes;

    • Name of student(s)
    • Relationship to student
    • Whether the student knows a report is being filed

  6. In the section titled “Behavior of concern” describe the concern, circumstances under which the concerning behavior was observed, and any steps to address that may have already been taken.

  7. Click the green Save button in the upper-right hand corner of the screen.
  8. A confirmation screen will appear, indicating that your report record has been submitted.
  9. Resolve ticket.
