Troubleshoot + Repair

No one joining your meeting

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Check that the invitation contained the correct URL.
  3. Verify meeting is scheduled for the correct time zone.
  4. Host - contact attendees to rule out technical issues.
  5. Resolve ticket.

Participant can't log in

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Verify that the participant is using the correct password.
  3. Ask the participant to open a new browser window and copy and paste the meeting URL from the invitation email into the browser Address bar.
  4. Resolve ticket.

WebEx session loads slowly

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Ask the participant to log off and log back in again.
  3. Ask the participant to close all applications except WebEx.
  4. Have the participant clear the Web browser cache and delete temporary files and cookies.
  5. Verify that the issue is not with the participantâ€™s ISP by asking them to test a Web page they access frequently.
  6. Resolve ticket.

Participant's teleconference disconnects

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Host - send a chat or an instant message to the participant and ask them to rejoin the audio conference by clicking the Audio button and selecting a phone or VoIP option.
  3. Resolve ticket.

Your browser crashes (host)

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Open a new browser window and try to rejoin the meeting using the meeting number and Host ID.
  3. If you cannot rejoin, stay on the phone.
  4. Have backup material that you can read over the phone, or ask your alternate host to present.
  5. Resolve ticket.

You are hearing hold music

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Use the audio indicator in the Participants panel to identify whose line it is, then mute that participant.
  3. Host - send the participant a chat message asking to take you off hold.
  4. Host - explain what happened, and explain how participants can mute their own connections.
  5. Resolve ticket.

Participant's cannot hear one another

back to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. Make sure that audio connections are not muted.
  3. Host - have participants check their phone or VoIP connections and confirm that microphones or headsets are powered and switched on.
  4. Try to reset a VoIP connection by clicking Mute then Unmute.
  5. Resolve ticket.

Unable to Resolve

back to top

Any ticket (except an outage) that cannot be resolved by an Analyst should be assigned to Deskside (DSS). Also, All callers should be reminded that as a platinum customer, he or she is entitled to request in-person service at any time.

Escalate all outage reports as described below. A Service Desk Analyst should never contact the vendor or open a vendor support ticket for any reason.

  1. Verify user identity.
  2. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  3. Note any troubleshooting steps taken.
  4. Describe the problem.
  5. Assign ticket to DSS resolver group.
