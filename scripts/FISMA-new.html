FISMA FCSRE

The FISMA Compliant Secure Research Environment (FCSRE) is a controlled environment and FISMA-based security program. Research data with sensitive content may be stored and analyzed inside the environment.

The Federal Information Security Act of 2002 (FISMA) is a law requiring protection of sensitive data created, stored or accessed by the Federal Government or any entity on its behalf.

Tools for Resolution

Required Information

  • Name
  • CWRU Network ID
  • Contact information (preferred and alternate)
  • User status with respect to SRE (provisioned, applied, pre-application, de-provisioned)
  • User role with respect to SRE (Research Personnel - on-campus, Clinical/Field Personnel, Partner/Vendor, System Administrative, Application Management, Compliance)
  • User role within SRE org structure (tenant, user, management team, ops team, advisory)
  • Tenant / Organization name
  • Problem or Request description

Access Prerequisites

  • Active CWRU NetID
  • Active Duo Security account
  • Approval from Tenant Manager or Study Official
  • Completed training
  • Signed confidentiality agreement (via SRE Manager)
  • Employees - HR clearance (via SRE Manager)

Resolver Groups

SRE Admin: Available by appointment Mon.-Fri., 8 a.m - 5 p.m., EST. Inquiries placed before 5 p.m. will be acknowledged same-day. Customers should expect a response within 3 business days.

Tenant Resolvers: One resolver group per established tenant. The tenant resolver will resolve all issues not handled by the service desk that are specific to that tenant, or if unresolved, will assign to SRE Admin.

How to Escalate

Who to contact for escalation

FISMA Compliant Secure Research Environment planning is not considered a critical service. Issues that may in other cases be escalated should be assigned to the SRE Admin resolver group.

Description and purpose

An escalation may be requested by an end user when he or she determines that a critical issue has not been acknowledged properly, remains unresolved for an unacceptable period of time or is severe enough that it requires atypical handling.

Escalation should not be confused with assignment to a resolver group. An escalated issue is typically brought to the attention of management within a second-level support group(s).

Escalation procedures

Escalation is not available at this time. Assign all unresolved issues to the SRE Admin resolver group.

Activate + Setup + Install

New user

return to top

  1. Gather resolution information and enter it into the ticket.
  2. New users must complete a SRE Access Request Form (paper), which is provided them by a Study / Tenant Official.
  3. The SRE Access Request Form includes requested entitlements and an expiration date.
  4. The SRE Access Request Form must be signed by both the end user and his or her Study / Tenant Official.
  5. User should contact his or her Study Official to request this form.
  6. In addition to issuing request form, a Study / Tenant Official will;
    • verify user identity
    • approve or deny study access
    • approve or deny request to create SRE account
    • verify user has met training requirements
  7. Upon receipt of a completed and approved form, the SRE Manager will verify the validity of the form and generate a work ticket for the SRE Operations team.
  8. Tickets for the SRE Operations team may not be created on the basis of an initial end-user request.
  9. To resolve general questions about the process of requesting access, including pre-award consultation, onboarding, cost and assistance seeking funds, please see Contact Research Computing.
  10. Resolve ticket.

    Tactical
    Data containing personal identifiers, including clinical data from affiliated hospitals, is used in research by CWRU faculty, instructors and students. This data needs to be protected as required in federal and state regulations.

    A fully-FISMA compliant research environment provides competitive advantage to CWRU researchers seeking funding through grants and contracts.

Outage Management Procedure (PROC40) - Summary

  1. A network or service outage, or degradation of a critical service will either be found through monitoring or communication to the NOC.
  2. The NOC will reach out to the resolver team and create a P0/P1 prioritized ticket based on feedback from the team, and communicate the outage and available details to a predetermined group of people by email. A bridge line will be open with dial in information provided in the communication.
  3. NOC agents will reach out to the resolver team periodically for updates on the status of the outage as well as an ETA on resolution to the problem. These updates will be sent out to the predetermined communication recipients and provided on the bridge line to anyone who calls in for an update.
  4. Upon resolution of the outage, the NOC will communicate that information to the email recipients and within 2 hours generate a Problem Record for Root Cause Analysis (PROC24).
